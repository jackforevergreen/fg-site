# Forestry Restoration in the Eastern Plains of Colombia: Rebuilding Ecosystems and Communities

![Forestry Restoration](/post/forestry-restoration-in-the-eastern-plains-of-colombia-rebuilding-ecosystems-and-communities/fri1.jpg)

**Project Name:** Forestry Restoration in Productive and Biological Corridors

**Location:** High Orinoco region, Eastern Plains of Colombia

**Project Type:** Afforestation & Reforestation

**Annual CO2 Reduction:** 92,660 tCO2e

**Project Standard:** CDM - AR-ACM0003 (Afforestation and reforestation of lands except wetlands)

The Forestry Restoration in Productive and Biological Corridors project aims to restore natural forests and create sustainable forest production systems in the High Orinoco region of Colombia. This project targets land previously used for extensive cattle ranching, transforming it into productive forests that support biodiversity and create biological corridors. Through reforestation efforts, the project seeks to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by sequestering carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. Over its 60-year duration, the project is expected to reduce or remove 5,559,630 tons of CO2, resulting in an annual reduction of 92,660 tons of CO2.

## Environmental and Social Contributions

The project contributes to environmental restoration by promoting the growth of new forests in previously degraded areas. These forests help capture carbon dioxide, thus reducing overall greenhouse gas concentrations. Additionally, the project supports biodiversity by creating habitats for various species and improving the ecological balance of the region. On the social front, the project involves multiple stakeholders, including local organizations such as Organización La Primavera S.A. and Bosques de la Orinoquía S.A. It generates sustainable economic opportunities for local communities through job creation and the promotion of sustainable land use practices. This includes the development of commercial forest plantations and the restoration of natural forests, which provide financial, social, and environmental benefits to the region.

## Impact and Certification

The project is certified under the Clean Development Mechanism (CDM) and adheres to the AR-ACM0003 methodology for afforestation and reforestation. It aims to deliver significant carbon reductions while contributing to the sustainable development goals (SDGs) by promoting healthy lives, economic growth, and reduced inequalities in the region.

## Expanding the Vision for Sustainable Development

The success of the Forestry Restoration project in the Eastern Plains of Colombia serves as a compelling example of how environmental restoration can also drive socio-economic transformation. The project not only enhances the ecological health of the High Orinoco region but also uplifts local communities by providing them with sustainable livelihoods. The combination of innovative land-use strategies and the utilization of carbon markets offers a replicable model for other regions facing similar challenges. By drawing labor away from illicit activities and toward productive, environmentally beneficial work, the project is contributing to a long-term vision of sustainable development that balances environmental, social, and economic priorities.
