# How Can I Offset my Flight Emissions?

In today's world, air travel is often unavoidable. Whether it's for work, vacation, or visiting family and friends, flying offers unparalleled convenience. But with that convenience comes a significant environmental cost. Flights are a major contributor to global carbon emissions, which have a lasting impact on our planet. The good news is, while we may not be able to eliminate air travel, we *can* take meaningful steps to reduce its impact by [offsetting our emissions with Forevergreen](https://www.forevergreen.earth/flight-offset).

## The Carbon Footprint of Air Travel

The aviation industry is responsible for about 2.5% of global carbon emissions, and the number continues to rise as more people fly each year. On a per-passenger basis, air travel generates a considerable amount of CO2. For example, a round-trip flight from New York to London emits approximately **2.4 metric tons** of CO2 per passenger, which is equivalent to the emissions from driving a car for **6 months**.

Even short flights can significantly contribute to your personal carbon footprint. Flights are often among the largest contributors to an individual's yearly emissions. Yet, reducing our reliance on air travel isn't always realistic. So, what can we do?

## What Does It Mean to Offset Your Emissions?

Carbon offsets provide a way to neutralize the impact of your emissions by funding projects that reduce carbon elsewhere. When you purchase a carbon offset through Forevergreen, the money goes directly toward verified projects like reforestation, renewable energy, and carbon capture technologies. These projects remove or reduce CO2 from the atmosphere, compensating for the emissions you can't avoid.

In essence, offsets allow you to take responsibility for your emissions and actively contribute to solutions that are helping fight climate change.
