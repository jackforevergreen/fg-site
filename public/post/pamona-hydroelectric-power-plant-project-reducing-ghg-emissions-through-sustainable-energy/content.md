# Pamona Hydroelectric Power Plant Project: Reducing GHG Emissions through Sustainable Energy

![Pamona](/post/pamona-hydroelectric-power-plant-project-reducing-ghg-emissions-through-sustainable-energy/php1.jpg)

**Project Name:** Pamona 2 Hydroelectric Power Plant Project

**Location:** Central Sulawesi Province, Indonesia

**Project Owner:** PT Poso Energy

**Project Type:** Hydroelectric Power Plant

**Annual CO2 Reduction:** 1,024,074 tCO2e

**Project Standard:** Clean Development Mechanism (CDM)

**Certification:** WCD Compliant

The Pamona 2 Hydroelectric Power Plant is a 195 MW run-of-river project located on the Poso River in Central Sulawesi, Indonesia. This project generates zero-emission power without the need for a dam, supplying electricity to the West, South, and Southeast Sulawesi grids. The plant contributes to reducing greenhouse gas emissions by displacing electricity that would otherwise be generated by fossil fuel power plants.

The project began operations in 2012 and has since achieved an annual reduction of 1,024,074 tCO2e. The baseline scenario assumed that, in the absence of the project, electricity would have continued to be produced by fossil fuel power plants. The monitoring and verification (MRV) of the project involve tracking the net electricity supplied to the grid, in accordance with national requirements for electricity generation monitoring.

## Project Details

The Pamona 2 Hydroelectric Power Plant operates with three vertical shaft Francis turbines, each generating 65 MW of electricity. The project is designed as a run-of-river plant, which means it utilizes the natural flow of the river without requiring large reservoirs or dams. This design minimizes environmental impact and avoids the displacement of communities, a common issue with dam-based hydroelectric projects. The project boundary includes the CO2 emissions from the fossil fuel-fired power plants that have been displaced due to the Pamona project. This ensures accurate calculation and certification of the greenhouse gas reductions achieved.

## Environmental and Social Contributions

The Pamona project provides significant environmental benefits by reducing reliance on fossil fuels and lowering greenhouse gas emissions. In addition to contributing to global climate goals, the project supports local sustainable development through the creation of jobs during both the construction and operational phases. Infrastructure improvements, such as road construction and the renovation of local facilities, have further enhanced the quality of life for the surrounding communities. The project also includes social programs, such as providing free electricity to the adjacent village, which has encouraged the growth of small industries and improved living standards in the area.

## Monitoring and Verification

The monitoring of the Pamona project involves tracking the gross electricity generation, auxiliary electricity consumption, and net electricity delivered to the grid. These measurements are essential for calculating the actual greenhouse gas emissions reductions, which are verified by independent third-party auditors to ensure compliance with the Clean Development Mechanism (CDM) standards.
